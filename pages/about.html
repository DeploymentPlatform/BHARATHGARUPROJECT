About Trinity Power Solutions

Building dependable energy infrastructure since 2019.

Our Journey

Established in Hyderabad, Serving India

Trinity Power Solutions was established in Hyderabad, India, in 2019 with a vision to deliver dependable, innovative, and future-ready power solutions for industrial, commercial, and residential sectors. We began our operations as an authorized channel partner with leading global power brands, including Luminous, APC, BPE, Fuji Electric, V-Ctrl Solutions Pvt. Ltd., Elnova, Exide Industries, and Amaron.

Recognizing the growing complexities in power networks and the need for localized integration, we expanded into manufacturing and turnkey project execution in 2021. This expansion allowed TPS to build extensive custom capabilities across power conditioning, electrical distribution infrastructure, transformer solutions, and solar EPC execution.

Today, TPS delivers end-to-end energy infrastructure solutions focused on reliability, efficiency, sustainability, and long-term technical support. Our integrated model ensures that our clients receive seamless, optimized power layouts backed by certified technical experts.

Our Operational Timeline

How we grew from a regional distributor to an integrated infrastructure provider.

2019

Foundation & Partnering

Trinity Power Solutions established in Hyderabad, India. Secured authorized channel partnership alliances with leading manufacturers like Luminous, APC, BPE, Fuji Electric, Exide, and Amaron to supply high-quality power backup hardware.

2021

Manufacturing & Integration Expansion

Expanded footprint into customized manufacturing and turnkey project execution. Built engineering facilities to assemble LT panels, APFC panels, and perform industrial-grade electrical distribution integrations.

Present

Integrated Infrastructure Leadership

TPS stands as a single-point contractor executing power conditioning, high-voltage transformer solutions, customized electrical panels, and solar EPC projects ranging from residential rooftops to 10 MW industrial solar plants.
